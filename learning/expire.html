Will My Options Expire?

Understand the concept of expiration dates and their impact on your options.

Video Lesson

Podcast

In-depth Text Format

Yes, options do expire. Every stock option contract has a specific expiration date. This is the final day on which the option holder can exercise their right to buy or sell the underlying asset at the strike price.

If an option is not exercised or sold before its expiration date, it becomes worthless. This is a key risk of options trading—the value of the option is tied to both the price of the underlying stock and the time remaining until expiration. As an option nears its expiration date, its "time value" decreases rapidly, a phenomenon known as time decay.

For most stock options, the expiration date falls on the third Friday of the expiration month. It is critical for an investor to be aware of an option's expiration date to avoid losing their entire investment.

Knowledge Check

Question: What happens to an option that is not exercised or sold before its expiration date?

Answer: The option expires worthless, and the investor loses the premium they paid for it.
