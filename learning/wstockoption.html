What is a Stock Option?

A foundational lesson on what stock options are and how they differ from stocks.

Video Lesson

Podcast

In-depth Text Format

A stock option is a contract that gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell a stock at a predetermined price, known as the strike price, before a certain date, the expiration date.

This is a key distinction from owning a stock itself. When you buy a stock, you own a piece of the company. When you buy a stock option, you own the right to a potential transaction in the future. There are two main types of stock options:

  • A call option gives you the right to buy a stock at the strike price. This is used when you believe the stock's price will go up.
  • A put option gives you the right to sell a stock at the strike price. This is used when you believe the stock's price will go down.

Investors use stock options for two main purposes: to speculate on a stock's future price movement or to hedge (protect) an existing position against potential losses.

Knowledge Check

Question: What is the main difference between owning a stock and owning a stock option?

Answer: Owning a stock is a direct form of ownership in a company, while owning a stock option is a contractual right to buy or sell a stock at a specific price in the future.
