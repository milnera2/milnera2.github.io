What is a Stock?

A foundational lesson on what a stock is and its role in the financial market.

Video Lesson

Podcast

In-depth Text Format

A stock (also known as equity or a share) is a type of security that signifies ownership in a corporation and represents a claim on part of the corporation's earnings and assets. When you buy a company's stock, you become a part-owner of that company.

The value of a stock can change based on a company's performance, economic conditions, and market sentiment. By owning stock, investors have the potential to earn a profit in two primary ways: through dividends (a portion of the company's earnings distributed to shareholders) or through an increase in the stock's price, which can be realized when the stock is sold.

Investing in stocks is a key component of building wealth over the long term, but it also comes with risks, as the value of stocks can go down as well as up.

Knowledge Check

Question: What does owning a stock in a company signify?

Answer: Owning a stock signifies part-ownership in a corporation, giving you a claim on its assets and earnings.
