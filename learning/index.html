Options Trading

Options trading involves buying or selling contracts that give the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price within a specified time frame. It's a strategy used to speculate on price movements or hedge existing investments.

Key Concepts

attach_money

Strike Price

The price at which the option holder can buy or sell the underlying asset.

calendar_month

Expiration Date

The date by which the option must be exercised or it expires.

trending_up

Call Option

The right to buy an asset at the strike price.

trending_down

Put Option

The right to sell an asset at the strike price.

Strategies

show_chart

Long Call

Buying a call option when expecting the asset price to rise.

show_chart

Long Put

Buying a put option when expecting the asset price to fall.

show_chart

Short Call

Selling a call option when expecting the asset price to stay below the strike price.

show_chart

Short Put

Selling a put option when expecting the asset price to stay above the strike price.

Risks

warning

Options can expire worthless, leading to a total loss of the premium paid.

warning

Options trading can be complex and requires a good understanding of market dynamics.

warning

Short options strategies have unlimited risk if the asset price moves significantly.
