What does portfolio diversity mean?

Explore the concept of diversification and why it's a crucial strategy for managing risk.

Video Lesson

Podcast

In-depth Text Format

**Portfolio diversification** is the strategic process of spreading your investments across various assets, industries, and geographical regions. The core principle behind diversification is to reduce the risk of your portfolio by ensuring that a poor performance in one area is offset by positive performance in another.

Think of it as the old adage, "Don't put all your eggs in one basket." If you invest all your money in a single company's stock, your entire investment is vulnerable to that company's specific risks. A sudden downturn, a product failure, or a negative news report could wipe out your entire investment.

By diversifying, you hold a mix of assetsâ€”such as stocks, bonds, real estate, and commodities. Within stocks, you can further diversify by investing in different sectors (e.g., technology, healthcare, energy) or different-sized companies (large-cap, small-cap). This approach helps to smooth out your investment returns and provides a more stable long-term growth path.

Knowledge Check

Question: What is the main goal of diversifying a portfolio?

Answer: The main goal of diversifying a portfolio is to **reduce risk** by spreading investments across different asset classes and sectors.
